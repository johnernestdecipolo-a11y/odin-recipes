Nes' Signature Lasagna Recipe

The Meat Sauce

  • 1 lb (450g) Ground beef (or a mix of beef and Italian sausage)
  • 1 Medium onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 28 oz (800g) Crushed tomatoes or marinara sauce
  • 2 tbsp Tomato paste
  • 1 tsp Dried oregano and dried basil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

The Cheese Filling

  • 15 oz (425g) Ricotta cheese
  • 1 Egg (to bind the cheese so it doesn't spill out)
  • 1/2 cup Grated Parmesan
  • 1/2 tsp Nutmeg (optional, but adds professional depth)

The Assembly

  • 9–12 Lasagna noodles (boiled or "no-boil" variety)
  • 3 cups Shredded Mozzarella cheese
  1. Prepare the Sauce

    In a large skillet or pot, brown the meat over medium-high heat. Drain the excess fat. Add the onions and cook until soft (about 5 minutes). Stir in the garlic, tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, and herbs. Simmer on low for at least 20 minutes to thicken.

  2. Mix the Ricotta

    In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta, egg, Parmesan, and a pinch of salt. Mix until smooth.

  3. Layer the Lasagna

    Use a 9x13 inch baking dish. Follow this specific order to ensure the bottom noodles don't stick:

    1. Sauce: Spread a thin layer on the bottom of the dish.
    2. Noodles: Lay 3–4 noodles across.
    3. Ricotta: Spread 1/3 of the mixture over the noodles.
    4. Mozzarella: Sprinkle a handful of cheese.
    5. Sauce: Pour 1/3 of the meat sauce.
    6. Repeat: Repeat the layers (Noodles -> Ricotta -> Mozzarella -> Sauce) until you reach the top.
    7. Final Top: The last layer should be Noodles covered in Sauce and a heavy layer of Mozzarella.
  4. Bake
    • Preheat oven to 190°C (375°F).
    • Cover the dish tightly with foil. Bake for 25 minutes.
    • Remove the foil and bake for another 20–25 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  5. The Critical "Rest" Period

    Let the lasagna sit for 15 minutes before cutting. If you cut it immediately, the layers will slide apart. Resting allows the cheese to set and the noodles to absorb the excess moisture.
