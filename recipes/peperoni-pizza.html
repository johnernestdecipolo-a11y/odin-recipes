Pepperoni Pizza

Nes' Signature Pepperoni Pizza Recipe

The Dough

  • 2 cups All-purpose or "00" flour
  • 3/4 cup Warm water (around 40°C)
  • 1 tsp Active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1/2 cup Pizza sauce (thick is better)
  • 1.5 cups Shredded Mozzarella (low-moisture is best)
  • 20–30 slices Pepperoni
  • Optional: Dried oregano or a drizzle of honey for a "hot honey" finish.
  1. Activate the Yeast

    Mix the warm water, sugar, and yeast in a small bowl. Let it sit for about 5–10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This proves the yeast is alive and ready to work.

  2. Knead the Dough

    In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix until a dough forms, then knead on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  3. The First Rise

    Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm spot for 1 to 1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  4. Shape the Pizza

    Preheat your oven to its highest possible setting (usually 250°C to 260°C). If you have a pizza stone, put it in now. Punch the dough down and stretch it into a circle on a piece of parchment paper. Do not use a rolling pin if you want a bubbly crust; use your hands to push the air from the center to the edges.

  5. Layering
    1. Sauce: Spread a thin layer, leaving about an inch for the crust.
    2. Cheese: Distribute the mozzarella evenly.
    3. Pepperoni: Lay the slices on top. Putting them on top of the cheese allows them to get crispy and "cup" up in the heat.
  6. Bake

    Slide the pizza into the oven. Bake for 8–12 minutes. Watch for the crust to turn golden brown and the cheese to blister.

  7. The Finish

    Let it sit for 2-3 minutes before slicing. This prevents the cheese from sliding off immediately.
